Grizzlies Defeat Bucks, 132-119, Completing Season Sweep

The Memphis Grizzlies (33-16) defeated the Milwaukee Bucks (26-21), 132-119, on Sunday at Fiserv Forum. Memphis, who have won seven of their last eight games, swept the season series against Milwaukee with a 2-0 record.

Jaren Jackson Jr. scored 37 points, including seven 3s to pace the Grizzlies, his seventh straight 20-point game. Santi Aldama scored 23 points off the bench with eight rebounds, while Desmond Bane had 22 points and grabbed nine rebounds. Zach Edey notched his sixth career double-double with 14 points and 11 rebounds.

Giannis Antetokounmpo led the Bucks with 30 points and 11 rebounds. Khris Middleton contributed 20 points off the bench. Brook Lopez scored 15 points, and Damian Lillard tallied 15 points with nine assists.

Jackson Jr. scored 16 in the fourth quarter to help Memphis outscore Milwaukee 39-20 in the final frame. Scotty Pippen Jr. recorded eight of his 10 assists in the fourth quarter, the most in a single final period in franchise history. The Grizzlies made 15 of their 27 shots in the fourth.

The Grizzlies return to FedExForum to face the San Antonio Spurs on Monday, February 3, at 7 p.m. CT. The game will be televised on FanDuel Sports Network Southeast and broadcast on 92.9 FM ESPN Radio.
